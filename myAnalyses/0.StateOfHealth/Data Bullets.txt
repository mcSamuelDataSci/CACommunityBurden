* The overall health of Californian's has been steadily improving and is good.
* Life expectancy is a key measure of population heath and summarizes health over the life course into one number—it is at its highest level ever.  Life expectancy in 2018 is estimated to be 84.4 years for females and 79.5 year for males.
* data from 2020 Fusion/DOF collaborative estimates
* slide X, and in CCB with estimates for (almost) all counties
* A parallel measure, "all cause death rate" is at its lowest level ever, and has decreased substantially and steadily for males and females, for all racial/ethnic groups, and in all regions of the State.
* While overall death rates have decreased for all group, they have decreased faster for some than others.  One consequence of these changes is that “rate ratio”, a key measure of disparity, of the all-cause death rate for blacks to whites has decreased over the past two decades, indicating this key disparity is decreasing.
* see slides X, and CCB trend tab
* Infant mortality, a key overall measure of the health of a population, particularly the health and prenatal health care of mothers, is at its lowest rate ever -- 4.2 infant deaths per 1,000 live births in 2017
* https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/pressroom/states/california/california.htm)

* This overall improvement in health is due to steady and sustained decreases in some of the leading causes of death.  
* Ischemic heart disease, by far the leading killer of Californians, has decreased steadily and substantially over recent decades, including the last two decades; this decrease has been experienced by males and females, all race/ethnic groups, and all age groups
* Deaths from some other types of cardiovascular disease, notably stroke, have also decreased
* result of better diets; better medicine/medical care
* Very strong and steady decreases in death from cancer overall, and from several leading type of cancer, including Lung cancer, Colon cancer and Breast cancer. Combination of public health and medicine
* There have also been some noteworthy decreases in some important, but less common conditions.
* Deaths from COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) have also decrease substantially
* There have been large decreases in Hepatitis C deaths (over four-fold decrease in males!) in past decade (following large increases in the decade before that)
* This is largely due to new, highly effective medications, and public health's role educating about medication and getting
* Deaths from Hepatitis B have also been steadily decreasing
* likely do to vaccination

* Large decreases in deaths from (lower) respiratory infection most of last two decades
* Decreases in road injury deaths in the first decade of this century

* Life Course
* Age is, of course, strongly associated morbidity and mortality. Death rates, and many disease rates, are much higher in older age groups compared to younger groups. For example, while persons > 85 years of age are only x% of the California population, they account for xx% of all deaths.  
* Deaths in the older age groups are heavily dominated by chronic diseases, including cardiovascular disease, Alzheimer’s disease, and cancer.
* While the numbers of deaths and serious diseases are much smaller among younger persons, these occurrences are intrinsically and centrally important to Public Health, and all of society, because of (1) the emotional impact and human suffering associated with death, disease, and disability of young people; (2) the large number of years of premature death contributed by deaths among younger people, and (3) the economic and other societal loses due to death and disease among persons in their most (economically) productive years. 
* Deaths in younger persons are dominated by deaths in highly preventable “injury-related” conditions including, drug-associated deaths, road injuries, suicide and homicide.
* Require different intervention strategies
* And, events impacting children in their very earliest years, including Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) are well documented to increase the risks for a myriad of  … SDOH … poor outcomes in those early years as well as throughout the life course. 

* Mental Health is a critically important, often overlooked, area of Public Health. Mental Health conditions are leading causes of Years Lived with Disability and leading causes of Hospitalizations
* Mental disorders are a leading cause of Years Lived with disability (second only to musculoskeletal disorders) based on estimates for California
* 
* Mood disorders, Schizophrenia, and other psychotic disorders are among the largest (non-birth/pregnancy) causes of (in-patient) hospitalizations overall (after only septicemia), and are the largest reason for hospitalizations in all age groups from 5-14 to 35-44  
* See AGE - https://skylab.dev.cdph.ca.gov/content/33/
* In addition to these direct measures, mental health is inextricably and directly related to a wide range of other conditions and factors including suicide, substance use/abuse, homelessness, ACES and poor educational/economic attainment; and less directly, but nevertheless importantly related to many others.

* Many large disparities exist in the health of Californians.  Addressing these unacceptable disparities in a key focus of Public Health.  
* Disparities in health, in access to health care, and in upstream social/structural determinants of health, are found between many sectors of society, including sex, gender, and sexual orientation; age; rurality; immigration status; race/ethnicity; and other categories.  Disparities (and inequities) in heath between racial/ethnic groups are especially apparent, preventable, and the subject of much public heath investigation and action.  Too many disparities to even summarize or rank here, but….
* Disparities in all-cause death rates between non-Hispanic Blacks and non-Hispanic Whites are profound, deeply troubling, and exemplary of immediate outcomes and the impact of a life-course of disparities.  Overall, the age-adjusted all-cause death rate is 30% higher (rate ratio of 1.3) for Blacks than Whites. AND, as bad as this is, it obscures the shocking disparity in deaths rates between Black and White young, middle aged, and even elderly persons.  The death rate for children 0-4 year of age is 2.6 time higher for Blacks than whites; 2.0 times higher for children age 5-14 year of age; and higher in Blacks than Whites in every age group through 75-84 years of age!  Only in the very oldest age group, 85 years of age and older, is the Black rate lower that the White rate, reflecting a complex cascade of deaths in younger age groups, and a “hardiness” survivorship phenomenon.
* 

* Beyond this “all-cause” view, crucial disparities and insight are seen looking at cause-and age group-specific disparities between Black and Whites. For eleven causes of death, for persons <75 years of age, the Black death rate is greater than twice as much as the White rate; for several of these the rate is more than 3 times higher, and for homicide the rate is almost 8 time higher. In relation to the very earliest stage of the life course, it is striking to note that deaths from neonatal conditions are 3.6 times higher for Blacks than Whites, as are deaths related to maternal conditions.

* Constant possibility of explosive epidemics -- in the 2018-2019 season, 615 influenza-coded deaths were identified, 246 influenza outbreaks were reported, and there were about 20,000 positive influenza results at sentinel labs around the State.  These represent only a fraction of the cases that occurred, AND are a small fraction of the cases that could occur from a novel strain and/or from a unique mode of transmission -- https://www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CID/DCDC/CDPH%20Document%20Library/Immunization/Annual2018-19.pdf
* adolescent birth rate (ABR) continues to decline, to a new record low of 13.9 births per 1,000 females between the ages of 15 and 19. Those numbers reflect an 11.5 percent decline between 2016 and 2017. Declining rates can be attributed to a number of factors, including improved access to reproductive health services and increased use of contraception; delayed first sexual intercourse; and public health prevention, education and support programs.
