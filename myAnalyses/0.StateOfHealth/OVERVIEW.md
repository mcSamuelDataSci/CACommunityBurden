####  1. The **overall** health of Californian's is great


* Life expectancy, a key measure of population heath, summarizing health over the life course into one number, is at its highest level ever -- life expectancy in 2018 is estimated to be 84.4 years for females and 79.5 year for males.
  * data from 2020 Fusion/DOF estimates
  * slide X -- CCB has estimales for (almost) all counties
* A paralel mesaure, "all cause mortaily" is at its lowest rate ever, and has decreased substantailly and steadily over for males and females, for all racial/ethnic groups, and in all regions of the State
  * while overall death rates have decreased for all group, they have decrased faster from some than others.  One consequene of these changes is that the **disparity** in the rate ratio (a key measure of disparity) for black to white all-cause deaths has decreased over the past two decades. 
  * see slides X and CCB trend tab
* Infant mortaility, a key overall mesaure of the health of a population, particularly the health and prenatal health care of mothers, is at it's lowest rate ever -- 4.2 infant deaths per 1,000 live births in 2017
  * https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/pressroom/states/california/california.htm)


#### 2. Steady and sustained decreases in some of the leading casues of death, and some other noteworthy conditions

* Ischemic heart disease, by far the leading killer of Californias, has decreased steadily and substantilly over recent decades, including the last two decades; this decrease has been experienced by males and females, all race/ethnic groups, and all age groups
* Deaths from some other types of cardiovascualr disease, notabley Strokes, have also decrased

* Very strong and steady decreases in death from cancer overall, and from several leading type of cancer, including Lung cancer, Colon cancer and Breast Cancer. Combination of public health and medicine
* Deaths from COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) have also decrease substantailly


* There have been large decreases in Hepatits C deaths (over four-fold decrease in males!) in past decade (following large increases in the decade before that)
